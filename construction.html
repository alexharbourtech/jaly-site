Our Process
01

Knowledge via Metering

Understand your energy usage down to the minute and identify areas for savings by installing a small number of utility meters.

02

Control

Install control devices — smart relays — for identified device categories. Every device is controllable on-site or remotely and includes power monitoring.

03

Automate

Schedule devices based on time or other criteria. Automations can be easily overridden on-site if required.

The Problem

Cabin Heaters

Always On

Heaters running constantly, all night, all weekend and when unoccupied during different project phases — left on "just in case".

Transformers

Always On

Large transformers staying energised after hours because turning them off trips the breaker. So nobody does.

No Visibility

No Data

No knowledge of the excess and out-of-hours power use, so costs are just accepted. Without data there is no starting point.

The Solution

Cabin Heaters

Scheduled

Heating runs to site hours with manual boost available. Hardwired wall buttons give workers on-demand warmth for a set period. Cabins are the correct temperature at the right time.

Transformers

Automated

Transformers controlled by time and/or load with staggered starts and modern MCBs to avoid startup issues. Switched off after hours, back on before the crew arrives.

Fleet Dashboard

Immediate

Savings begin the moment the devices are installed. Web-based portal for visibility and control of all devices across multiple sites. Payback is typically seen in 2–4 weeks.

The Result

Measurable Benefits

What started as a hunch has identified up to €1,500 in weekly savings per site. Benefits are immediate with payback from 1–4 weeks typically.

Sustainability

Overall reduced energy consumption regardless of source — batteries, generators or grid. Supports ESG goals and commitments across your estate.

Scaleable Solution

Proof of value can be achieved via a single site area, reducing initial investment and de-risking the decision before a full rollout.

Our Methods

Fleet Dashboard

Web-based portal for visibility and control of all devices across multiple sites. Easily see what is in use and switch it off. Automations and control can be per device, group, site, or globally.

Heating Controls

Control and automate heating infrastructure to ensure energy efficiency and reduce waste in unused areas and out of hours. Monitor drying rooms for issues and required operation.

Transformer Management

Large transformers are often left online 24/7. Automating their operating times can save thousands of euros per month across a site estate.

Lighting

Sensor lights are common but not always practical. Automating certain controls and switching off mass devices when not required enables a more efficient and sustainable site.

Case Study

Irish Tier 1 Contractor

Our client suspected high out-of-hours electricity usage and reached out seeking assistance in identifying and reducing this wastage. A single meter was supplied to prove the wasted volume and calculate potential savings.

Following this, they tested relay devices for proof of concept and subsequently commenced a pilot site ahead of a full rollout.

The full project scope includes over 50 sites, with annual savings estimated to be in the millions. Jaly are providing Design, Supply and Commissioning Services for this project.

Regulatory

BACS Regulations — already in force

Non-residential buildings with heating, cooling, and ventilation systems above 290kW were required to install Building Automation and Control Systems by 31 December 2024 under S.I. 393 of 2021.

A properly designed and commissioned Jaly system delivers BACS-compliant functionality. There are numerous other use cases and potential benefits we can explore with you.
