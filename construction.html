The Problem

Out-of-hours energy waste is a construction sector norm.

Always On
Cabin Heaters

Electric wall heaters in site cabins run constantly — all night, all weekend, and during shutdown periods. They are left on “just in case” because turning them off and on requires someone to be there. Nobody is.

Always Energised
Transformers

Large 10kVA transformers stay live after hours because switching them off trips the breaker on restart. Without a solution to that problem, the path of least resistance is to leave them on. All weekend. Every weekend.

No Data
No Visibility

Without metering, there is no baseline and no way to quantify waste. Energy costs are absorbed into the project budget and never interrogated. The problem persists because nobody can prove its scale.

The Solution

Automate, control, and measure — at site and fleet scale.

Cabin Heating — Automated to Site Hours

Shelly relays at the fused spur of each heater put every cabin on a schedule. Heating runs when the site is occupied and stops when it is not. For early starts or ad-hoc use, a hardwired boost button beside each heater gives workers on-demand warmth for a set period — no app, no login, no friction. Remote triggering is also available: cabins can be switched on before a crew arrives via a simple notification or message.

Transformer Control — Off When Not Needed

Shelly controls transformer switching by time, or by time and load. Transformers power down after hours and restart in a staggered sequence each morning to prevent the load spike that causes breaker trips. Modern MCBs resolve the compatibility issue entirely — a straightforward one-off change that makes automated switching safe and reliable.

Metering — Prove the Saving Before You Spend

A single energy meter on the site incomer gives the full picture of out-of-hours consumption within days of installation. Additional meters per cabin or cabin group provide granular visibility. Water meters layer in alongside power data. Everything appears in one dashboard — real time, remotely accessible, no site visit required.

  • Patterns visible within days of installation
  • Quantify waste before committing to full rollout
  • Data to build the business case internally
Fleet Dashboard — Every Site, One Interface

Shelly Fleet Manager provides centralised control and visibility across every site simultaneously. Control per device, per group, per site, or across the full estate. Automations apply at fleet scale. Every action is logged. Human override is always available — at device level, group level, or site level. The system works around the crew, not against it.

Regulatory Context

BACS regulations are already in force.

Irish law (S.I. 393 of 2021, transposing the EU Energy Performance of Buildings Directive) requires non-residential buildings with combined heating, cooling, and ventilation output above 290 kW to have a Building Automation and Control System installed. The compliance deadline was 31 December 2024.

For construction companies with large site offices and accommodation, this threshold is likely to be relevant across the estate. The compliance assessment requires a feasibility study by an independent competent professional.

A properly designed and commissioned Jaly system delivers BACS-compliant functionality — putting you on the right side of the regulation while generating a direct commercial return.

Feasibility Threshold

The economic feasibility test for BACS installation is a payback period of 3 years for commercial buildings. At €1,500 per site per week in identified savings, the Jaly construction solution achieves payback in weeks — clearing this threshold by an order of magnitude.

EN ISO 52120-1:2022 — BACS Classification

A
High energy performance automation

Advanced BAC with full technical building management. Predictive control, demand response, detailed reporting.

B
Advanced automation

Advanced BAC with some technical building management. Energy monitoring, automated scheduling, fleet control.

C
Standard automation — minimum compliant

Standard BAC with centralised monitoring and automated control. The minimum required by the EPBD Regulations. A Jaly system meets or exceeds this.

D
Non-compliant — must be replaced

Manual or non-energy-efficient controls. New buildings cannot be built with Class D systems. Existing buildings must be upgraded.

Case Study

Tier One Irish Building Contractor — National Rollout

The Engagement
From a hunch to a national programme.

A tier one Irish building contractor identified that out-of-hours electricity use across its site network was likely significant, but had no data to quantify it. Jaly supplied a single energy meter to a representative site. Within days, the scale of out-of-hours consumption was confirmed and savings were calculated.

Following proof of concept, relay devices were tested on a pilot cabin group to validate the control approach. A pilot site of ten cabins was then commissioned to refine the system ahead of full rollout.

The full programme covers 50+ sites nationally. Jaly is providing system design, hardware supply, and commissioning services throughout. Over 4,000 Shelly devices are planned across metering, relay control, and switching infrastructure.
