Construction · Fleet Energy Management

Tier One Contractor — National Site Network

"We have transformers running all weekend on sites that are empty. Nobody has ever turned them off."

A tier one Irish building contractor identified significant out-of-hours energy waste across its national site network. Site cabins with electric heating, and large 10kVA transformers that power tools during the working day — nothing was being switched off.

Jaly designed and is deploying a fleet-managed system covering every site. In-wall relays at the fused spur of each cabin heater give granular heating control per device. A specific MCB solution allows transformers to be switched safely without tripping — powered down after hours, brought back in a staggered sequence each morning to prevent load spikes.

A custom dashboard presents energy data and control in the format the client's operations team requires — per device, per group, per site, or across the full estate.

Active deployment — scaling nationally

Residential · Whole-Home System

Private Residence — Cork

"I want the whole house smart — heating, power, everything — and I want it all in one app."

A comprehensive whole-home brief. The challenge in projects like this is the breadth — gas central heating, electric underfloor heating, mains power circuits, environmental sensors, access control — each of which would ordinarily require a separate platform.

Smart relays for power and lighting circuits throughout the property. Electric heaters and underfloor mats controlled at the spur. Smart TRVs manage gas-fired radiators room by room. Water metering, smart door lock, and smoke detection complete the system. Every device in the same app, no separate logins.

Energy Infrastructure · Social Impact · Grid

National Grid Demand Management

"Wind farms are being switched off at night because there's too much energy on the grid. Homes in energy poverty can't afford to heat their water."

When wind generation exceeds grid demand — most commonly overnight — the grid operator instructs wind farms to curtail output, incurring fines. Meanwhile, households in energy poverty ration basic utilities including hot water.

A Shelly relay fitted in parallel with the existing immersion heater in each qualifying home. When surplus wind energy is available, devices are triggered remotely to heat water at no cost to the household. Energy metered per MPRN; bill credit applied automatically. Future state: predictive triggering five to ten minutes before curtailment instructions.

Entering pilot — scaling to hundreds of homes

What Was Deployed

  • Shelly relay in parallel with domestic immersion heaters
  • Remote trigger infrastructure — fleet-scale activation
  • Per-device energy metering linked to household MPRN
  • Automated bill credit on energy consumed
  • Grid curtailment signal integration
  • Future: predictive triggering 5–10 minutes pre-curtailment

Hospitality · Mixed-System Heating

Hotel Heating — 100-Room Property

"We have gas radiators, electric wall heaters, and underfloor mats — three different systems with no central control."

A 100-room hotel with three distinct heating technologies. Smart TRVs on gas radiators connected via USB gateways in corridor-mounted cabinets. In-spur relays control electric heaters. Relays manage underfloor mats. All three under one Fleet Manager dashboard.

Where the hotel's property management system can be linked, room heating follows occupancy automatically. Common areas run on schedules set by hotel management. First Irish deployment of this configuration.

In development — first Irish deployment

What Is Being Deployed

  • Smart TRVs on gas-fired radiators — per room
  • USB gateway solution in corridor cabinets
  • In-spur relays for electric wall heaters
  • Relays for electric underfloor heating mats
  • Fleet Manager — single dashboard for all heating types
  • Occupancy system integration — heating follows room status
  • Energy monitoring per room type
